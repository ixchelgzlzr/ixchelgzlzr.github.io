I have taught courses ranging from introductory biology, evolutionary biology, phylogenetics, and computational methods. I am especially interested in making phylogenetic and macroevolutionary tools more accessible through practical workshops with reproducible code. 

Below I list some of the courses with avilable repositories for anybody to access.

## Workshops and Tutorials

[**Divergence time estimation using a Bayesian total-evidence dating approach**](https://ixchelgzlzr.github.io/TED_and_Paleobotany/about/)

Developed in collaboration with Rocio Deanna and Stacey Smith, this workshop introduces the foundations of divergence-time estimation using Bayesian total-evidence dating. The materials include background concepts and reproducible code for applying the model to empirical datasets.

This workshop was offered at Evolution 2022 and SSB 2023.
![Webpage screenshot]({{ 'assets/theme/images/solanaceae_page.png' | relative_url }})

***

[**Filobayes: un taller de filogenetica bayesiana en RevBayes**](https://ixchelgzlzr.github.io/filo_bayes_UNAM/)

Developed with Rosana Zenil-Ferguson and Santiago Ramirez-Barahona, this Spanish-language workshop introduces Bayesian phylogenetics using RevBayes. The materials are designed for a three-day practical course and include examples, exercises, and teaching resources.

This workshop was offered in January 2025 at the Instituto de Biologia, UNAM.

![Workshop group picture]({{ 'assets/theme/images/group_filobayes.png' | relative_url }})

***

[**Spatial Phylogenetics in R (English version)**]({{ '/resources/tutorial.html' | relative_url }})

[Spanish version]({{ '/resources/tutorial_spanish.html' | relative_url }})

This tutorial introduces spatial phylogenetic analyses with the R package `phylospatial`, with materials available in English and Spanish. It covers the basic workflow for connecting phylogenetic information with geographic patterns of biodiversity.
